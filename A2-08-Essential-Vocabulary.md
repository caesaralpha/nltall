### Telling Time
| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Hoe laat is het? | What time is it? |
| Het is drie uur. | It's three o'clock. |
| Het is half vier. | It's half past three (3:30). |
| Het is kwart over vijf. | It's quarter past five. |
| Het is kwart voor zes. | It's quarter to six. |
| Het is tien over acht. | It's ten past eight. |
| Het is tien voor negen. | It's ten to nine. |

**Remember:** "Half" in Dutch refers to half TO the next hour!
- Half drie = 2:30 (NOT 3:30)

## Practice: Build Your Vocabulary

1. Learn 10 new words per day
2. Make flashcards
3. Practice with native content (news, podcasts)
4. Use words in sentences
5. Review regularly

---

## Key Points to Remember

✅ Learn article (de/het) with every noun  
✅ Practice time expressions for everyday use  
✅ Master numbers for shopping and dates  
✅ Learn verbs with their prepositions  
✅ Use weather vocabulary in daily small talk
